Getting Involved in Scientific Research: Finding a Lab

Learning about Opportunities via Email

Research an institution – whether it be universities, hospitals, or government institutions – near where you live that conducts your research of interest is a good start. The best way to inquire about research opportunities is by email. The email address of the person who is responsible are usually stated on the institution’s webpage. Sometimes, professors at universities will reject or not respond to your request, thus, it is recommended that your email multiple professors. In you email, include your name, current school, previous experience/science background, reason for seeking this research opportunity, research interest, and résumé. In your email, you can write about a recent scientific paper that the professor published to express your interest and increase your chances of being offered a research opportunity!

Finding Opportunities through Personal Connections

If you know friends, family, or former graduates at your school who have worked in a lab or local universities, they can be a valuable source of information for research opportunities. In addition, Guidance Counsellors might be able to provide advice based on the experience of former students who have acquired such opportunities.

Applying to Summer Programs

There is a myriad of summer programs for high school students that can give them direct access to labs for hands-on experiences. For more information on specific science programs for high school students and how to apply, visit Programs, Competitions, and Volunteering Opportunities page.
